If you have used twitter recently, you may have clicked a link to a gallery. It was probably titled something like "10 worst celebrity tatoos" or "50 pictures of silly dogs". These galleries are great for filling in time when you have a few minutes to spare like while waiting for the bus to arrive. While I do enjoy mindless entertainment sometimes, I would also like to do something constructive with my "in between" time. Infotreats is a gallery site that teaches you something small each time but can add up over time. Whether it's history or physics or business, making galleries that are educational could be a great way to get interested in a new subject. Subjects will come in series with links to the next gallery at the end of the sequence so that users can easily continue on or stop at any time.

So how will this business model work? Well by ads of course. These gallery sites (see "Likes.com") are designed for mobile ad revenue. Some are making $1000 dollars a day. My goal is to get to $1000 dollars a week within six months. I put $1000 dollars down as my bitstarter goal. The money will be used to buy tweets from people with large amounts of followers in order to jump start the galleries. One company followed this exact model and was making $3000 a day within seven days after launching. These gallery sites are here to stay and I want to help make them a more constructive use of time. I'll update soon with incentives for investing.
